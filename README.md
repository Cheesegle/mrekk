# A Different Kind of PP

## An osu! Omegaverse AU Fanfiction

---

The first thing mrekk noticed was the smell.

Not the usual smell of his apartment — the stale energy drinks, the mechanical keyboard oil, the faint vanilla candle his roommate insisted on — but something *deeper*. Something that made his alpha hindbrain sit up and take notice like he'd just seen a DT play worth 900pp.

He blinked at his second monitor, where Discord was open. A message from Akolibed glowed on the screen:

**akolibed:** yo you good? you've been sniffing the air like a dog for the past 5 minutes on stream

**akolibed:** chat is concerned

mrekk's ears — his actual ears, the soft floppy ones that emerged during his last heat — flattened against his head. He hadn't even realized he was doing it. His tail, curled around the leg of his gaming chair, gave a nervous twitch.

**mrekk:** im fine
**mrekk:** just
**mrekk:** something smells really good??

He saved and quit, closing osu! mid-map. His stream would understand. Probably. Chat would spam "L" but chat always spammed "L."

The smell was coming from the kitchen.

---

It was *him*.

Not him-him. Not mrekk. The smell was coming from *himself*, which made absolutely no sense unless—

mrekk stood frozen in the kitchen doorway, his hand hovering over the fridge handle. His omega instincts — the ones he'd been suppressing with scent-blockers and willpower and the sheer stubborn refusal to acknowledge his secondary gender since he presented at fifteen — were *screaming*.

"No," he said out loud to the empty apartment. "No, no, no."

His stomach flipped. Not in the "I just choked a 7-star jump pattern" way. In the *other* way. The way it had been flipping on and off for the past two weeks, which he'd attributed to bad takeout and the stress of the osu! World Cup.

He looked down at his stomach.

Flat. Same as always. Thin and pale from spending sixteen hours a day in a chair.

But *warm*.

His hand pressed against his lower belly without conscious thought, and something inside him — something that felt distinctly *not* him — pulsed back.

"Oh, *fuck*."

He was not buying a pregnancy test right now.

He was buying *three* pregnancy tests, because the first one came back positive and he assumed it was a false positive, and the second one came back positive and he assumed the whole box was defective, and the third one—

Positive.

mrekk sat on the bathroom floor with his back against the tub, his ears drooping, his tail wrapped around his waist like a comfort blanket. Three pink lines. Three positive signs. Three very clear indicators that his suppressants had stopped working sometime around the OWC qualifiers and his body had decided to go fully offline without notifying management.

The father—

He closed his eyes.

The father was not relevant.

The father was a tournament organizer from a different region who had been in the same hotel during OWC. They'd shared a hallway, then a conversation, then a heat-rut collision that mrekk had categorized under "things that happen when your scent-blockers fail at 2 AM in a hotel with shared ventilation."

He didn't even have the guy's number. Just a first name and the vague memory of hands that were too gentle for an alpha and a voice that had whispered *let me take care of you* like it was the most natural thing in the world.

His phone buzzed.

Akolibed arrived forty minutes later with two bags of groceries, a heating pad, and the specific expression of someone who had just realized their friend was about to go through something that no amount of osu! skill could click through.

"Okay," Akolibed said, dumping the bags on the kitchen counter. "Talk to me."

mrekk, who had migrated from the bathroom floor to the couch and was currently wrapped in a blanket like a very small, very stressed burrito, said: "I don't know where to start."

"Start with why you never told anyone you're an omega."

"Because it doesn't matter."

"It clearly matters—"

"In osu! it doesn't matter. The circle doesn't care about your secondary gender. The pp doesn't care. If I play well, I play well, and if people knew I was an omega, every conversation about my skill would become *'well he's an omega so actually his consistency is impressive because omegas typically—'*" He made a frustrated noise, ears twitching. "I didn't want the asterisk."

Akolibed sat down on the other end of the couch. "...That's fair."

"I've been on suppressants since I was fifteen. My scent is blocked. My heats are suppressed. I've been functioning as a beta for six years and it was *fine*."

"Until it wasn't."

"Until it wasn't."

The heating pad hummed to life. mrekk pressed it against his stomach and immediately felt his entire body go liquid with relief. A small, involuntary whine escaped his throat, and he clapped a hand over his mouth.

"Bro," Akolibed said gently. "You don't have to hide that stuff around me."

"I've been hiding it for six years. It's kind of my whole thing."

"Well, your whole thing is going to get a lot harder when you're waddling."

mrekk threw a pillow at him.

The hard part wasn't the pregnancy. The hard part was the *playing*.

"WHAT THE FUCK," mrekk screamed at his monitor, as his cursor sailed harmlessly through empty space and missed a jump that a Silver player could hit. "I can READ. I can SEE the note. My HAND won't—"

His hand was trembling. Not from carpal tunnel, not from nerves, but from the hormonal soup that his body was currently marinating in like a slow cooker set to *produce a whole-ass human*.

He'd switched from DT to NM. Then from NM to HD. Then from HD to EZ, and now he was considering if there was a mod called "PLEASE JUST LET ME PLAY THE CIRCLES" that would account for the fact that his fine motor control was being hijacked by an embryo the size of a blueberry.

Week six: The nausea started.

mrekk had always thought "morning sickness" was a misleading term, and he was right. It was *all-day sickness*. It was *I-just-set-a-new-personal-best-and-now-I-need-to-vomit sickness*. It was *the-sound-of-circles-clicking-makes-me-gag sickness*.

He played through it. Of course he did. He was mrekk. He'd played through wrist pain, through migraines, through the deep existential dread of being #1 in a game that demanded everything. He could play through nausea.

Except when he couldn't.

He was mid-stream, three hours in, going for a top play on a 6.5★ map, when the smell of his own keyboard — the plastic, the finger oils, the faint metallic tang of the switches — hit him like a truck, and he barely made it to the bathroom before his lunch made a return appearance.

He came back to his desk pale, ears pinned, tail limp.

Chat was concerned now. Genuinely concerned.

**user1:** yo is he sick?
**user2:** he just ran off camera
**user3:** mrekk please take care of yourself
**user4:** has he been eating?

He ended stream early.

**Akolibed:** you good?

**mrekk:** I don't think I can hide this much longer

It wasn't a question. It wasn't even a statement of possibility. It was the quiet, terrifying acknowledgment that his body was going to make choices with or without his consent, and the body had decided on *parenthood*.

**Akolibed:** then don't hide it

Week eight: The cravings.

"I need strawberries," mrekk said at 3 AM, standing in the kitchen in boxers and an oversized hoodie, his ears perked forward with a predatory intensity usually reserved for finding the cursor on a 10-star tech map.

"We have strawberries," Akolibed said from the couch, where he was scrolling through his phone.

"The WRONG strawberries. I need the strawberries from that specific market across town. The organic ones. With the green stems still on."

"mrekk, it's 3 AM."

"I can taste them. In my MIND. I can taste strawberries that don't exist yet and I need them to EXIST."

Akolibed looked up from his phone. mrekk was gripping the counter with both hands, and there was a genuine, almost feral desperation in his eyes that had nothing to do with pp and everything to do with the tiny collection of cells currently rewriting his entire brain chemistry.

"...I'll drive."

"You're a good friend."

"I'm an enabler is what I am."

Week ten: The reveal.

It happened on accident.

He was streaming, doing a casual playthrough of new ranked maps, and he'd been doing fine — no nausea, no trembling hands, just the clean rhythm of tap and flow that had made him the best in the world. His audience was relaxed, chat was moving at a comfortable pace, and for a moment, everything felt normal.

Then a donor asked: **"mrekk, are you an omega? Your ears have been showing a lot lately."**

He froze. On camera. In front of fifteen thousand people.

His ears — which had indeed been more visible lately, because the suppressants that kept his omega traits suppressed had been gradually losing effectiveness as his body prioritized the pregnancy — were fully out. Soft, brown, slightly too big for his head. They twitched visibly when he read the message.

Chat *erupted*.

**user1:** WAIT
**user2:** OMEGA????
**user3:** HE HAS EARS
**user4:** I KNEW IT
**user5:** omg they're so cute???
**user6:** mrekk omega confirmed
**user7:** this changes nothing about his skill wtf
**user8:** does this mean—
**user9:** PUPPY EARS PUPPY EARS PUPPY EARS

mrekk sat in silence for ten seconds. Then twenty. His tail, which he'd been hiding under his desk for weeks, curled around his ankle.

"Okay," he said, voice flat. "Yes. I'm an omega. This is not new information that affects anything. My rank is still my rank. My plays are still my plays. The circles are still the circles."

He took a breath.

"And also I'm pregnant."

The chat broke.

Completely. It went from a flowing river of text to a waterfall of pure chaos. Emotes, keysmashes, congratulations, confusion, disbelief — all of it flooding the screen so fast that the bot couldn't keep up.

mrekk watched it happen with a strange sense of calm. The secret was out. The thing he'd been holding — the weight of it, the fear of it, the *shame* he'd been taught to feel about it — dissolved in the flood of reactions, and what was left underneath was just... him. An omega. A player. A person with floppy ears and a puppy tail and a tiny life growing inside him, who still loved osu! more than almost anything in the world.

"Anyway," he said, "I'm going to keep playing. If you're here to be weird about omega players, leave. If you're here to watch me click circles, stay."

He loaded the next map.

Chat, miraculously, settled.

And mrekk played.

Week twelve: The father.

mrekk was in the middle of a nap — pregnancy naps were non-negotiable, his body simply *shut down* at random intervals like a laptop with a failing battery — when his phone rang.

He answered without looking.

"What."

"Um." A voice. Deep, warm, with an accent he recognized immediately. "This is... this is going to sound insane. I'm the tournament organizer from OWC. We, um. We met in the hallway."

mrekk sat up so fast his head spun. "How did you get my number?"

"Akolibed."

"I'm going to kill him."

"He said you would say that. He also said you wouldn't actually do it because—" A pause. "Because you need the pack support right now."

mrekk's eyes stung. Stupid hormones.

"What do you want?" he asked, but his voice came out softer than he intended.

"I saw your stream. The announcement. And I... I can do math, mrekk. The timeline—"

"It's not—" mrekk started, then stopped. Because it *was*. They both knew it was.

"I'm not going to demand anything," the voice said quickly. "I'm not going to show up at your door or file a claim or any of that alpha bullshit. I just... I wanted you to know that I'm here. If you want me to be. And if you don't, that's okay too."

The silence stretched between them, crackling over the phone line.

"You don't even know me," mrekk whispered.

"I know you click circles better than anyone in the world. I know you have the softest ears I've ever seen. I know you fell asleep on my shoulder that night and you made a tiny whistling sound when you breathed, and I thought—" A rough exhale. "I thought you were the most beautiful thing I'd ever seen."

mrekk's ears went flat against his skull. His tail curled tight around his waist.

"I'm not good at this," he said. "At... people. At feelings. At anything that isn't osu!."

"Neither am I."

A pause.

"What's your name?" mrekk asked. "Your full name. I only have the first one."

"Marcus."

"Marcus," mrekk repeated, tasting the word. It fit. It fit the hands and the voice and the *let me take care of you*.

"Hi, Marcus," he said quietly. "I'm pregnant with your baby and I play a rhythm game for a living and I'm kind of a mess."

A laugh, warm and startled. "Hi, mrekk. I know."

---

Week sixteen: The comeback.

The tournament was in two months. His belly was just starting to curve beneath his oversized shirts, a gentle swell that his audience had started affectionately referring to as "the pp bump." His ears were always out now. His tail was always visible. He'd stopped hiding, and the world hadn't ended.

His plays were getting better. Not because the pregnancy had given him some magical advantage, but because the *stress* of hiding had lifted, and underneath it was a player who still had the fastest fingers in the game and now also had a reason to take care of himself.

"DT on this one?" chat suggested.

mrekk looked at the map. 7.2★. Fast streams, big jumps, a pattern that would have been terrifying a month ago when his hands wouldn't stop shaking.

He selected Double Time.

"Let's go," he said.

His ears perked forward. His tail wagged once, just once, behind him. His hand hovered over the tablet, steady and sure.

The map started.

And mrekk — omega, player, future parent, still the best in the world — clicked every single circle.
